## NEUTRAL (7 cards)

*Neutral cards have no playstyle allegiance. They fill gaps, provide utility, and work in any build. Every player will use some neutral cards — they're the glue that holds specialized builds together.*

### Firing Cards

| Card | Bullets | Dmg | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard Round | 6 | 8 | White | None. Baseline. |
| Piercing Round | 6 | 8 | Blue | Passes through first target. |
| Ricochet Round | 6 | 7 | Steel Blue | Bounces once off walls toward nearest enemy (10m). |
| Drain Round | 6 | 5 | Dark Red | Heals player 2 HP per hit. |

**Standard Round** — The baseline. Exists so players have a frame of reference for what "normal" damage feels like. Every other card is measured against this. You want to REPLACE these, not collect them. The fact that you can't remove Standards from your deck creates the deck-thinning desire that drives deckbuilding decisions.

**Piercing Round** — Simple utility that rewards positioning. If enemies line up, you get double value. Teaches players to think about enemy arrangement, not just "shoot the closest one." Works in any build because it's just "your bullet but it hits two things."

**Ricochet Round** — Rewards spatial awareness. Players who learn to use walls get value from this. Creates moments of "I shot the wall and killed the guy behind cover" — shareable, clip-worthy. Also a soft counter to Shield Bearer enemies (future) who block frontal attacks.

**Drain Round** — The survivability card for any build. Lower damage (5 vs 8 baseline) is the cost. Creates a real tradeoff: "Do I pick Drain for survival or a damage card for killing faster?" This card lets aggressive players stay aggressive by healing through shooting rather than hiding.

### Function Cards

| Card | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Barrier | Cyan | 30 HP shield, 5 sec. |
| Vampiric Burst | Dark Red | 3 sec: gun damage heals 50% of damage dealt. |
| Reload Surge | Near-White | Instant reload. Skip 2 sec delay. Both hands reshuffle. |

**Barrier** — The panic button. When Rushers are closing and you're at low HP, pop Barrier and survive. In the starter deck because new players NEED a defensive option to learn the game without dying every 30 seconds. Simple, readable, always useful.

**Vampiric Burst** — The aggressive healer. Unlike Barrier (which is defensive — shield up and hide), this rewards SHOOTING. Pop it and then fire into enemies — the more damage you deal, the more you heal. Synergizes with POWER cards (Heavy Round + Vampiric Burst = huge heal per shot) and FLUX cards (Overclock + Vampiric Burst = many hits = many heals).

**Reload Surge** — The emergency reshuffle. Normally reload costs 2 seconds of vulnerability. This skips that. Use cases: (1) your magazine has bad cards, instant reshuffle for a better draw, (2) you just used all your spells and want them back NOW, (3) Rushers are closing and you can't afford 2 seconds of no shooting. This is the "undo" button for the deck system.

## POWER (8 cards)

*POWER is the "no gimmicks" playstyle. No status effects, no combos — just raw, massive damage per bullet. POWER players aim well and are rewarded with kills faster than any other build. The fantasy is "every shot is a bomb." The tradeoff: no status synergies, no AoE scaling, no sustain. You kill fast or you die.*

### Firing Cards

| Card | Bullets | Dmg | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Heavy Round | 4 | 15 | Dark Gold | High damage. Fewer bullets, each one HURTS. |
| Armor Piercer | 6 | 10 | Steel Gray | Ignores enemy damage resistance. |
| Headhunter Round | 6 | 8 | Crimson | Headshots deal 3x (instead of 1.5x). |
| Explosive Round | 3 | 12 | Burnt Orange | Each bullet explodes on impact. 3m AoE. |

**Heavy Round** — The signature POWER card. 15 damage per bullet (nearly 2x Standard) but only 4 bullets. Every miss hurts. This card rewards aim skill more than any other card in the game. At 3 shots/sec, the pack lasts ~1.3 seconds — fast and punchy. Combined with War Cry (+50%), each bullet does 22.5 damage. Combined with Megashot (5x on next bullet), one Heavy Round bullet does 75 damage — enough to one-shot a Grunt from across the arena.

**Armor Piercer** — Future-proofing card. Currently all enemies take full damage, but when armored enemies or Shield Bearers arrive, this becomes essential. Also exists as a "reliable 10 damage per bullet" option that's better than Standard without needing any setup. Good mid-tier pick for any build.

**Headhunter Round** — The skill ceiling card. 3x headshot means a player with perfect aim gets 24 damage per headshot (vs 12 with normal headshot multiplier). Bad aimers get 8 damage (same as Standard). This card ONLY rewards players who can consistently hit heads. It's the "git gud" card, and it should feel like a trophy pick.

**Explosive Round** — POWER's only AoE. 3 bullets that each create a 3m explosion. Against a group of Rushers, one pack can damage all of them. Only 3 bullets means the pack is short (~1 second) — you get a brief burst of AoE then it's over. This is POWER's answer to "what do I do against groups?" without needing status effects.

### Function Cards

| Card | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| War Cry | Gold | Next magazine: ALL firing cards +50% damage. |
| Iron Skin | Steel Gray | 40 HP shield, 6 sec. While active: +20% damage. |
| Megashot | Bright Gold | Next single bullet deals 5x damage. |
| Executioner | Dark Gold | Aim at target: instant kill if below 30% HP. Wasted if above. |

**War Cry** — The universal POWER multiplier. +50% damage to EVERY firing card in the next magazine. This is the card that makes POWER feel like POWER. Standard Round becomes 12 damage. Heavy Round becomes 22.5. Headhunter headshot becomes 36. It also works with OTHER playstyles — Venom Round with War Cry does 9 damage per bullet, making it decent damage AND poison stacking. This cross-playstyle utility makes War Cry one of the strongest cards in the game.

**Iron Skin** — The aggressive tank card. Unlike Barrier (pure defense), Iron Skin gives you a shield AND +20% damage while it's active. The design intent: you pop Iron Skin, charge into danger, and deal amplified damage while the shield absorbs hits. It's Barrier for players who don't want to play defensively. Synergizes with FLUX (dash in, pop Iron Skin, go fast + deal more).

**Megashot** — The sniper fantasy. One bullet, 5x damage. If your next bullet is Heavy Round, that's 75 damage in one click. If your next bullet is Headhunter Round and you hit the head, that's 8 × 5 × 3 = 120 damage. ONE BULLET. This is the "clip-worthy" card — the one players share screenshots of. The intent is to create "I just one-shot the Big Eye" stories. The tradeoff: it only affects ONE bullet, so if you miss, it's wasted.

**Executioner** — The finisher for when Detonator isn't in your build. Aim at a weakened enemy, press F, instant kill. But if the enemy is above 30% HP, the spell does nothing and you wasted it. The design creates a judgment call: "Is that enemy low enough? Do I risk it?" This is POWER's version of the 叠层后斩杀 — not stack-then-execute, but weaken-then-execute. It also hard-counters Big Eye (100 HP × 30% = need to get it below 30 HP, then one-shot it).

## VENOM (6 cards)

*VENOM is the stack-then-execute playstyle (叠层后斩杀). Patient setup → explosive payoff. You invest time stacking poison on targets, then detonate for massive burst. The fantasy is "I built up 30 stacks, then pressed one button and deleted the enemy." The tradeoff: slow damage output until the Detonator lands, and if you die before detonating, all that setup was wasted.*

### Firing Cards

| Card | Bullets | Dmg | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Venom Round | 6 | 6 | Bright Green | +2 poison stacks per hit. |
| Toxic Needle | 6 | 4 | Dark Green | +3 poison stacks per hit. Lower damage, fast stacking. |
| Plague Round | 4 | 6 | Yellow-Green | +2 poison stacks. On kill: spreads stacks to nearest enemy (8m). |

**Venom Round** — The core enabler. Every VENOM build starts here. 6 bullets × 2 stacks = 12 stacks per pack. Two packs of Venom = 24 stacks. Detonator on 24 stacks = 72 bonus damage (or 144 with Toxic Fire). This is in the starter deck because it teaches the stack-then-execute loop from minute one.

**Toxic Needle** — The "all in on stacking" card. +3 stacks per hit (vs Venom's +2) but only 4 damage per bullet. You're sacrificing immediate damage for faster stacking. A full Toxic Needle pack applies 18 stacks. Combined with Overclock (FLUX, 2x fire rate), you can stack 36 stacks in one pack duration. The intent: this card is for players who've committed to VENOM and want to go deeper.

**Plague Round** — The AoE VENOM card. On kill, poison spreads to the nearest enemy. This means killing a heavily poisoned Grunt can transfer 20+ stacks to the Big Eye nearby. The intent: make killing trash mobs valuable in a VENOM build — they become "stack batteries" you transfer to the real target. Only 4 bullets because the on-kill effect is so powerful.

### Function Cards

| Card | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Detonator | Purple | Hitscan. Consumes ALL poison stacks. Bonus = 3x stacks (6x if burning). |
| Toxin Bomb | Olive Green | All enemies within 8m gain 5 poison stacks. |
| Pandemic | Lime Green | All enemies within 10m gain 50% of highest poison stacks in range. |

**Detonator** — THE payoff card. The entire VENOM playstyle exists for this moment. You stack poison, you aim at the target, you press F, the stacks explode into damage. 3x multiplier means 20 stacks = 60 bonus damage. With Toxic Fire (target also burning), 20 stacks = 120 bonus damage. This is a function card (not a firing card) because the timing must be DELIBERATE — you choose exactly when to execute. It's hitscan (must aim and hit) because this is an FPS — the payoff moment should require skill, not just pressing a button.

**Toxin Bomb** — The area setup spell. 5 free stacks on everything within 8m. This accelerates the stacking phase — instead of shooting each enemy individually with Venom Rounds, you bomb them all at once, then Detonator the highest-stacked target. Also a combo starter: Toxin Bomb (5 stacks on all) → Pandemic (spread highest to all) → Detonator (execute the biggest).

**Pandemic** — The stack redistributor. If one enemy has 30 stacks and others have 5, Pandemic gives everyone 15 (50% of 30). This is the "I was stacking one target but now I want to AoE" pivot card. Also prevents "wasted stacks" — if you over-stack one target, Pandemic lets you spread the excess. Creates the fantasy of "poison spreading like a plague through the enemy group."

## BLAZE (6 cards)

*BLAZE is the area-damage amplification playstyle. Burn makes enemies take +20% damage from EVERYTHING. The fantasy is "the whole room is on fire and every bullet hits harder." BLAZE doesn't have its own finisher like Detonator — instead, it amplifies every other playstyle. VENOM + BLAZE = Toxic Fire. POWER + BLAZE = even bigger numbers. BLAZE is the best "second playstyle" to mix into any build.*

### Firing Cards

| Card | Bullets | Dmg | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Incendiary Round | 6 | 8 | Orange | Applies Burn (+20% dmg taken, 5 sec, refreshes). |
| Ember Round | 8 | 5 | Warm Orange | Applies Burn. 8 bullets for sustained uptime. |
| Magma Round | 4 | 10 | Deep Red | Applies Burn. Leaves fire patch (3m, 4 sec, 3 dmg/sec). |

**Incendiary Round** — The core BLAZE enabler. Same damage as Standard (8) plus applies Burn. There's almost no reason NOT to take this over Standard — it's strictly better. This is intentional. Incendiary is in the starter deck because Burn is the game's universal amplifier, and every player should experience it. The 5-second duration with refresh means as long as you keep hitting, the enemy stays burning.

**Ember Round** — The sustained burn card. 8 bullets (most of any card) means the longest pack duration. Low damage (5) but the point is keeping Burn active for a long time. In a BLAZE build, you want Burn to NEVER fall off — Ember Round's long pack ensures that. Also good as a "filler" card that does something useful without being exciting.

**Magma Round** — The area denial card. Fire patches on the ground persist for 4 seconds and damage enemies who walk through them. Against Rushers (who sprint toward you), dropping fire patches creates a deadly approach corridor. Also the only card that does damage after you stop shooting — the fire patches keep ticking. 4 bullets × 10 damage is good direct damage too. This card rewards players who think about positioning and enemy pathing.

### Function Cards

| Card | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Flashfire | Red-Orange | All enemies within 10m get Burn. |
| Inferno | Dark Red-Orange | All burning enemies take 30 instant damage. |
| Fuel | Amber | Next 3 firing card packs also apply Burn on hit. |

**Flashfire** — The instant area Burn. No aiming needed, just press F and everything nearby is burning. This is in the starter deck because it's the fastest way to activate Toxic Fire — Flashfire (burn all) → Venom Round (stack poison on burning targets) → Detonator (6x execute). It also works as a "pre-combat opener" — pop Flashfire at wave start, then everything you shoot takes +20% damage.

**Inferno** — BLAZE's own payoff card. 30 damage to every burning enemy. If 5 enemies are burning, that's 150 total damage from one button press. The more enemies burning, the better Inferno gets. This creates a BLAZE-specific playstyle: keep everything burning with Ember/Incendiary, then Inferno for burst. Unlike Detonator (single target execute), Inferno is AoE burst — it rewards spreading Burn widely rather than focusing one target.

**Fuel** — The cross-playstyle bridge card. "Next 3 packs also apply Burn" means ANY firing card temporarily becomes a BLAZE card. Venom Round + Fuel = bullets that apply BOTH poison AND burn. Heavy Round + Fuel = 15 damage bullets that also burn. This is the card that makes BLAZE the best "secondary playstyle" — you don't need many BLAZE cards, just Fuel, and suddenly your whole magazine is fire.

## FLUX (7 cards)

*FLUX is the speed/momentum playstyle. Move fast, shoot fast, chain hits, never stop. The fantasy is "I'm a blur of bullets and dodges." FLUX has the most function cards (4) because mobility IS spellcasting for this build. The tradeoff: low per-bullet damage. FLUX kills through volume and combos, not single big hits. Miss a shot and your momentum breaks.*

### Firing Cards

| Card | Bullets | Dmg | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Quicksilver Round | 8 | 5 | Light Silver | +20% move speed while firing this pack. |
| Tempo Round | 6 | 6 | Magenta | Consecutive hits within 0.5s: +2 bonus dmg each. Miss resets. |
| Tracer Round | 10 | 4 | Pink | Each hit gives next card pack +1 damage. Resets on reload. |

**Quicksilver Round** — The "run and gun" card. +20% move speed while firing means you're literally faster while shooting. Escape Rushers by running and shooting them simultaneously. Chase Big Eyes that try to keep distance. The movement bonus only applies during THIS pack, creating a rhythm: fast pack → normal pack → fast pack. 8 bullets means a longer-than-average pack duration, so you get extended mobility windows.

**Tempo Round** — The skill ceiling card for FLUX. Every consecutive hit adds +2 damage. First hit: 6. Second: 8. Third: 10. Fourth: 12. Fifth: 14. Sixth: 16. If you hit all 6 bullets: total = 66 damage (vs Standard's 48). But miss ONE shot and the counter resets. This card rewards consistent aim under pressure — the FLUX fantasy of "flow state" combat. Combined with Overclock (2x fire rate), the combo window gets tighter but the potential damage skyrockets.

**Tracer Round** — The "investment" card. Each hit gives the NEXT card pack +1 damage, persisting until reload. So if you hit all 10 bullets, the next pack does +10 damage per bullet. If the next card is Heavy Round: 15+10 = 25 damage per bullet × 4 bullets = 100 damage in one pack. Tracer rewards players who plan their magazine order — even though the order is random, knowing Tracer builds up value for whatever comes next creates anticipation.

### Function Cards

| Card | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Phase Dash | Light Gray | Instant dash forward 5m. Invincible during dash. |
| Overclock | Hot Pink | 6 sec: fire rate doubled (6 shots/sec). |
| Adrenaline | Pink | 6 sec: +40% move speed, +20% fire rate, +20% reload speed. |
| Blink | Light Blue | Teleport to crosshair position (max 15m). |

**Phase Dash** — The dodge card. When a Big Eye beam is coming or Rushers are in melee range, dash through them invincibly. 5m distance is enough to escape melee range. Can fire immediately after — so the pattern is: dash through enemy → spin around → shoot them in the back. This is the movement-as-combat card. Also useful for crossing open ground quickly between cover.

**Overclock** — FLUX's defining spell. 2x fire rate for 6 seconds turns 3 shots/sec into 6 shots/sec. Every firing card in your magazine gets doubled output. Venom Round with Overclock: 12 stacks per SECOND. Tempo Round with Overclock: combo counter builds twice as fast. Heavy Round with Overclock: 15 damage at machine-gun speed. This is the "build comes online" card — pop Overclock and feel the power spike.

**Adrenaline** — The "everything goes faster" card. +40% move, +20% fire rate, +20% reload. Less extreme than Overclock (which only does fire rate at 2x) but affects movement and reload too. This is the FLUX equivalent of War Cry — a universal buff that makes your whole toolkit better for a few seconds. Good for "I need to be faster at everything right now" moments.

**Blink** — The repositioning spell. Teleport to where your crosshair points, up to 15m. Unlike Phase Dash (forward only, short), Blink goes where you LOOK. Teleport to a platform above. Teleport behind the Big Eye. Teleport away from a Rusher swarm. This is the high-skill-ceiling movement card — creative players will find uses the designer didn't intend. Also the only card that lets you access elevated positions instantly.

## SHOCK (6 cards)

*SHOCK is the chain-reaction playstyle. Hit one enemy, damage two. Electrocute one, lightning bounces to three more. The fantasy is "one shot starts a cascade that wipes the room." SHOCK is strongest against groups and weakest against single targets (Big Eye alone). The tradeoff: chain damage is reduced per bounce, so it takes more setup to kill tanky enemies.*

### Firing Cards

| Card | Bullets | Dmg | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Volt Round | 6 | 7 | Blue | Hit chains to 1 nearby enemy (5m) for 50% damage. |
| Arc Round | 4 | 8 | Sky Blue | Hit chains to 2 nearby enemies (8m) for 40% damage each. |
| Static Round | 6 | 5 | Pale Blue | Applies Shock (3 sec). Shocked enemies take +15% chain damage. |

**Volt Round** — The core SHOCK enabler. Every bullet that hits chains to 1 nearby enemy for 50% damage. Against 2 enemies standing near each other, every Volt Round bullet does 7 + 3.5 = 10.5 effective damage. Against isolated enemies, it's just 7 (worse than Standard). This card teaches players to think about enemy clustering — the more grouped enemies are, the better SHOCK gets.

**Arc Round** — Volt Round's big brother. Chains to 2 enemies instead of 1, with wider range (8m). 4 bullets × (8 + 3.2 + 3.2) = 57.6 total effective damage against 3 grouped enemies. The shorter pack (4 bullets) means you get less time in "chain mode" but each bullet hits more targets. This is the crowd-clearing card — in wave 3 with 7 enemies, Arc Round is devastating.

**Static Round** — The Shock status enabler. Applies Shock for 3 seconds, which makes chain damage +15% stronger. The intent: Static Round → Volt Round is stronger than Volt Round alone. Static "primes" enemies for bigger chains. Also required for Chain Lightning (function card) to work — Chain Lightning only bounces off Shocked targets. Static Round is the setup, Chain Lightning is the payoff.

### Function Cards

| Card | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Chain Lightning | Blue | Hitscan. If target Shocked: bounces to 3 enemies, 15 dmg each. |
| Spotter | Red | Aim at target: apply Mark (+30% damage taken, 6 sec). |
| EMP Blast | Periwinkle | All enemies within 12m get Shocked + stunned 1 sec. |

**Chain Lightning** — SHOCK's payoff card (equivalent to Detonator for VENOM). Aim at a Shocked enemy, fire, lightning bounces to 3 more enemies for 15 damage each. Total potential: 15 × 3 = 45 damage spread across the room. If those targets are also Shocked (+15% chain damage), it's 51.75. Not as high single-target damage as Detonator, but hits MULTIPLE targets. This is the SHOCK build's "aha moment" — one spell clears half the room.

**Spotter** — The Mark applicator. Mark is a +30% damage debuff on one target for 6 seconds. This is the universal "focus fire" card — mark the Big Eye, then every bullet from any card does +30% more to it. Mark + Burn together = +20% (burn) + 30% (mark) = +50% damage amplification. Spotter works in any build but is categorized under SHOCK because Mark + Shock = Overload combo (future: explodes on death).

**EMP Blast** — The AoE crowd control + SHOCK setup. Every enemy within 12m gets Shocked AND stunned for 1 second. That's 1 second of no enemy attacks, no Rusher movement, no Big Eye beams. Then you follow up with Chain Lightning on any Shocked target for cascading damage. EMP Blast is SHOCK's opener — stun everything, then clean up. The 12m radius is large enough to catch most enemies in the arena.

## STARTER DECK (9 cards)

- 3x Standard Round (NEUTRAL, Firing) — *baseline, teaches basic shooting*
- 1x Venom Round (VENOM, Firing) — *introduces poison stacking*
- 1x Incendiary Round (BLAZE, Firing) — *introduces burn amplification*
- 1x Piercing Round (NEUTRAL, Firing) — *introduces utility, rewards positioning*
- 1x Detonator (VENOM, Function) — *introduces the 叠层后斩杀 execute moment*
- 1x Barrier (NEUTRAL, Function) — *defensive safety net for learning*
- 1x Flashfire (BLAZE, Function) — *introduces area spell, enables Toxic Fire combo*

**Starter deck design intent:** Exposes the player to 3 playstyles (NEUTRAL, VENOM, BLAZE) and 1 status combo (Toxic Fire) in their very first run. The first "aha moment" should happen naturally: player fires Venom Rounds at an enemy, fires Flashfire (burn all), then Detonator hits for 6x instead of 3x. "Wait, why did that do so much damage?" → discovery of Toxic Fire → "what OTHER combos exist?" → hooked.

## CROSS-PLAYSTYLE SYNERGIES

*These are the "1+1>2" discoveries. Designed so that mixing playstyles is often STRONGER than going pure.*

| Cards | From | Why it's exciting |
|---|---|---|
| Overclock + Toxic Needle | FLUX + VENOM | 6 shots/sec × 3 stacks = 18 stacks/sec. Stack an entire execute in 2 seconds. |
| War Cry + Heavy Round | POWER + POWER | 22.5 damage per bullet. Pure damage, no setup needed. |
| Fuel + Venom Round | BLAZE + VENOM | Bullets apply BOTH poison AND burn. One card pack enables Toxic Fire. |
| Overclock + Heavy Round | FLUX + POWER | 15 dmg at 6 shots/sec = 90 DPS for 6 seconds. Melts anything. |
| Flashfire + Detonator | BLAZE + VENOM | Burn everything → Detonator at 6x. The starter deck's designed combo. |
| Phase Dash + Explosive Round | FLUX + POWER | Dash into group → AoE explosions point blank. Aggressive mobility. |
| EMP Blast + Chain Lightning | SHOCK + SHOCK | Stun all → chain through all. Room clear in 2 button presses. |
| Adrenaline + Drain Round | FLUX + NEUTRAL | Fast movement + fast healing. Mobile sustain tank. |
| Megashot + Headhunter | POWER + POWER | 5x × 3x headshot = 15x damage one bullet. One-shot anything. |
| War Cry + Venom Round | POWER + VENOM | +50% damage on poison bullets. Decent DPS AND stacking simultaneously. |
| Fuel + Volt Round | BLAZE + SHOCK | Chain lightning spreads burn. Hit one enemy, burn two. |
| Pandemic + Inferno | VENOM + BLAZE | Spread poison to all → Inferno burns all → everything has poison+burn → Detonator any target for 6x. |
