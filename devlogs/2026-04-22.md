# Devlog: 2026-04-22 — Design Direction

## Starting Point

GP1 is a new project. Prior work on a Godot 4.6 extraction shooter (PES) informed early thinking — smooth 3C movement, PSX shader pipeline, and squad AI are concepts worth carrying forward. But GP1 is its own game, not a port.

---

## Finding the Hook

### The problem with "genre mashup" games
A game described as "X meets Y meets Z" has no identity. It borrows from references without owning anything. Commercial indie games need a **5-second trailer hook** — one idea strangers can understand without explanation.

### Directions explored and rejected

| Direction | Why rejected |
|---|---|
| Horror extraction | Smooth movement (which feels good) fights horror. Horror needs vulnerability. |
| Arcade extraction roguelite | No unique hook. Saturated market (Tarkov, Hunt, Marathon). |
| Backrooms extraction | "Backrooms: Extractions" on Steam has 10-100 concurrent. Trend peaked 2022. |
| HOLE-like weirdcore | Drops the shooter loop entirely. |

### The hook: FPS Roguelite Deckbuilder

Inspired by Slay the Spire. The "build" — constructing a powerful deck through choices — is the hook. Not combat, not atmosphere, not lore. **The build.**

**Evolved concept:**

> "Your deck is your magazine. Each shot fires a card."

This is structurally different from existing FPS roguelites (Gunfire Reborn, Roboquest) where cards/scrolls are passive modifiers. Here, cards are ACTIVE, CONSUMABLE, SEQUENTIAL. Every trigger pull is a micro-strategy moment. Reloading becomes reshuffling.

---

## "Cards Are Bullets" — Core Concept

### The pitch
Your deck is your magazine. Each shot fires a card. Reload to reshuffle. Build a powerful deck. Extract to keep it. Die and lose everything.

### What makes it unique
- Every trigger pull plays a card effect (not a passive modifier)
- Reloading = reshuffling the deck (transforms downtime into a strategic beat)
- Deck construction between fights determines combat options
- Strategy lives in the deck; reflexes live in the shooting
- Extraction risk/reward: bank your build or push deeper and risk losing it

### Two hooks combined
1. **"Your deck is your magazine"** — per-shot card effects, nobody has done this in FPS
2. **"Extract to keep your build"** — Tarkov risk/reward applied to deckbuilding

## Design Decisions

### Q1 — Card = bullet pack, fast pacing preserved
**Key evolution:** 1 card ≠ 1 bullet. 1 card = a PACK of bullets. Each card represents X bullets that all carry that card's effect. The deck shuffles card packs, not individual rounds.

**Why this works:**
- Fire rate stays fast (SMG, rifle, shotgun all viable at real FPS speeds)
- Card transitions happen every ~1.5-2.5 seconds regardless of weapon speed
- Player has time to notice the current "mode" (color shift), react, and play around it
- Magazine holds ~4-6 card packs across all weapon types

**Human visual processing research (cognitive science basis):**

| Player task | Time needed | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Detect a color change | ~100-150ms | Pre-attentive visual processing |
| Identify a color (red/green/blue) | ~150-200ms | Color recognition, near-instant |
| Recognize a simple icon/shape | ~200-300ms | Familiar symbols only |
| Read a single word | ~200-250ms | Trained readers, familiar words |
| Read a short phrase (2-3 words) | ~400-600ms | Eye fixation + comprehension |
| Read + understand a card description | ~1-2 sec | Parse text + apply to game state |
| Notice + adjust aim to different target | ~500ms | Attention shift + motor response |
| Notice + make a tactical choice | ~1-2 sec | Full decision loop |

**Design target: ~1.5-2.5 seconds per card pack.** This gives enough time to notice the mode, react, and enjoy it before switching.

**Bullets per card pack by weapon:**

| Weapon | Fire rate | Bullets per card | Time per card | Cards per mag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revolver | ~2/sec | 3-5 | 1.5-2.5 sec | 6 cards |
| Rifle | ~5/sec | 8-12 | 1.5-2.5 sec | 6 cards |
| Shotgun | ~1.5/sec | 2-3 blasts | 1.5-2 sec | 4 cards |
| SMG | ~8/sec | 15-20 | 2-2.5 sec | 6 cards |

**Key insight: all weapons have roughly the same TIME per card (~2 sec) despite different fire rates.** Fast weapons burn more bullets per card. The card system operates on a consistent pace.

**SMG is back on the table.** Fast fire rate is fine because cards don't cycle per bullet — they cycle per pack.

### Q2 — Card effects: Start grounded, escalate
- Basic cards = normal bullet projectiles (damage only)
- Uncommon = modified bullets (poison, ricochet, pierce)
- Rare = weird bullets (homing, split, gravity well)
- Legendary = beyond bullets (summon, area effect, time manipulation)
- Early game feels like FPS; late game feels like a card game manifested as shooting

### Q3 — Shuffle model: Random + progression-unlocked control
- Base state: random shuffle on every reload
- Progression unlocks partial deck control over time:
  - Perk: "pin" 1 card to always load in position 1
  - Perk: see the next 2-3 cards coming
  - Perk: "cycle" (skip current card, draw next)
- Never full control — always some randomness, always reactive

### Q4 — Card acquisition: Loot + purchase + extraction risk
- Run start: select initial perks (weapon + starter cards)
- During run: cards from loot drops and shop purchases
- **Extract via portal to keep your build. Die = lose everything.**
- "I found an amazing card. Do I extract now or push deeper?"

### Q5 — Card stacking: Multiple copies, low count
- 1-2 copies of a card max, not more
- High variety means players rarely see the same combo twice
- About building synergies from DIFFERENT cards, not stacking duplicates

### Q6 — Basic cards: Damage only, weapon-flavored
- Revolver basic = high damage, slow
- Rifle basic = moderate damage, moderate pace
- SMG basic = low damage, fast
- Shotgun basic = spread, close range
- Non-basic cards have abilities (poison, fire, ricochet, etc.)
- Deck thinning: as you collect better cards, basics get diluted naturally
- Open: can players actively REMOVE basics? (test in prototype)

### Q7 — Deck visibility: DECIDED
- **During combat:** the whole screen communicates the current card mode — muzzle flash color, bullet trail color, hit effects, crosshair tint, subtle screen edge vignette
- **Card icon on HUD is backup only** — player should know their mode from the visual language, not from reading UI
- **Card transition is the signal** — the visual shift when one card pack runs out and the next begins catches peripheral vision naturally
- **Weapon model:** shows remaining card packs as color-coded segments (e.g., revolver cylinder with colored chambers)
- **Between rooms:** full deck inspection, card details, tooltips
- **Design rule: during combat, the player SEES information through color and effects. They never READ information off a HUD panel.**
- Card pack duration (~1.5-2.5 sec) gives enough time for color recognition (~150ms) + reaction (~500ms) with room to spare

### Q8 — Weapons: Universal card loading + primal perks
- Any card goes in any weapon
- Each weapon has a unique primal perk that modifies how cards express:
  - Revolver: "First card each magazine deals 2x damage"
  - Rifle: "Every 3rd card triggers twice"
  - SMG: "Every 5th card triggers twice"
  - Shotgun: "Cards fire as 3 weaker copies in a spread"
- Weapon is the "lens"; card is the "signal"

## Weapon as Deck Strategy

This emerged from combining Q1 (card packs) + Q8 (primal perks):

| Build | Weapon | Cards/mag | Fire rate | Bullets/card | Identity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Precision | Revolver | 6 | ~2/sec | 3-5 | Each card pack is short, heavy hits |
| Balanced | Rifle | 6 | ~5/sec | 8-12 | Medium pace, room for combos |
| Chaos spray | SMG | 6 | ~8/sec | 15-20 | Fast, many bullets per card, effects everywhere |
| Area burst | Shotgun | 4 | ~1.5/sec | 2-3 blasts | Each card fires as spread copies |

All weapons cycle through card packs at roughly the same pace (~2 sec per card). Weapon choice determines how each card MODE expresses — few heavy bullets vs many light bullets — not how fast you move through the deck.

**Key design insight: the card system operates on a ~2 second heartbeat regardless of weapon speed.** This is derived from human visual processing — players need ~1.5-2.5 sec to notice a mode, react, and play around it.

## Card Design: Core Principles Decided

### Card-pack fundamentals
- 1 card = a PACK of bullets (not 1:1)
- Variable bullet count per card: strong cards = fewer bullets, weak cards = more bullets
- Packs are consumed on fire regardless of accuracy (miss = wasted bullets)
- Reload mid-pack: remaining bullets wasted, entire deck reshuffles
  - This makes reload a COST — creates decision: "use up this mediocre pack or sacrifice it for a reshuffle?"

### Card acquisition
- Loot drops: pick 1 from 3 (randomized). StS model. Meaningful choices.
- Each card set / archetype has its own perks and identity

### Core design philosophy: Stack-then-Execute (叠层后斩杀)
- Status effects are central to the design
- Pattern: apply stacks (poison, burn, etc.) with sustained fire → trigger a finisher card that consumes/detonates all stacks for massive damage
- This is the synergy engine — building toward an execute moment IS the deckbuilding goal
- Examples: 10 poison stacks + "Detonate" card = burst proportional to stacks. 5 burn stacks + "Cremate" = explosion scaled by stacks.

### Non-bullet effects (heal, shield, dash)
- Direction: these may live OUTSIDE the magazine as a separate perk/relic system
- Reasoning: heal/shield/dash don't fit naturally as "bullet packs"
- Mobility perks empower player movement as a system parallel to the card deck
- TBD: exact implementation

### Starter deck
- 70% basic cards, 30% non-basic
- For a 6-card magazine: 4 basic + 2 non-basic
- Non-basics should be interesting from the first magazine to hook the player

### Specific cards: NOT YET DESIGNED
- Card archetypes, specific effects, and synergies are TBD
- Will be designed around the stack-then-execute philosophy

## Identified Risks

1. **"Every shot feels the same"** — if 80% of cards are basics, 80% of shots are mundane
2. **Predictability vs chaos** — FPS players expect consistent guns; this deliberately breaks that
3. **Card-enemy balancing** — can't design "boss needs armor-piercing" if player might not draw it
4. **UI information overload** — FPS wants clean screen; deckbuilder needs information
5. **Content volume** — consumable cards need 200-300 designs for full game
6. **Balatro shadow** — card roguelite will be compared to Balatro; must own the FPS side
7. **Bad RNG frustration** — garbage draws before boss feels unfair (mitigate via deck thinning, progression perks)

## Competitive Landscape

### FPS Roguelites (validated market)
- **Gunfire Reborn** (4M+ copies) — scrolls as random passive modifiers, co-op
- **Roboquest** (Very Positive, ~50k reviews) — between-room card picks, stat bumps
- **ULTRAKILL** (~1M copies) — weapon variants, style meter, solo dev
- **Neon White** — cards ARE weapons (discard to ability), speedrun
- **BPM: Bullets Per Minute** — rhythm FPS roguelite

### Market gap
Nobody has done:
- Per-shot card effects (not passive modifiers)
- StS-style deliberate deck construction in FPS
- Dark/atmospheric aesthetic (most competitors are bright)
- Extraction risk/reward applied to deckbuilding
